The Wonders of Science

Momentum

Momentum is the product of mass and velocity. It describes the object's "quantity of motion". It is a vector quanitity and its direction is the same as its velocity. It's SI unit is kg x m/s.

Valence Electrons

Valence Electrons are electrons found on the outermost shell of the atom. In the periodic table, you can determine the number of valence electrons by looking at the group number. This also determines if the electron is stable or not.

Force

Force is the push and pull of an object. Force has maginitude and direction. It's SI unit is Newton
